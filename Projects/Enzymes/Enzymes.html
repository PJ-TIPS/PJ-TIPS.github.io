The Enzyme project

Guiding the modification of Cytochrome P450 BM3 toward biofuel production

Click here

In perfumes, γ and δ lactones are volatile fruit aroma compounds. These could potentially be made using a novel metabolic pathway being developed by the Patrik Jones group based, in part, on the use of a modified Cytochrome P450 BM3 (P450 BM3) enzyme. Michael Sternberg’s group at Imperial College aims to contribute to this project using evolutionary conservation tools to computationally guide P450 BM3 mutagenesis. This project classes these tools under the term “Evolutionary Conservation Software” and evaluates three examples in order to address the choice overload facing researchers. In addition, despite the proposed advantages of Evolutionary Conservation Software, there is a lack of clear methodology underlying its use in mutagenesis studies. To address the unclarity, this project established a new workflow using ConSurf (a software example), which leads to a reduction in potential mutagenesis sites along the substrate access channel of P450 BM3 by 13%. Ultimately, the methodology proposes the novel combination of mutations (Y51F, S72Y and L188Q) in order to alter regioselectivity and substrate specificity. These developments will contribute towards the renewable and natural production of lactones using synthetic biology, avoiding classical chemical synthesis pathways which are reliant on fossil fuels. Meanwhile, the workflow uses a variety of newly developed software and approaches which can be replicated and used alongside existing techniques to guide more automated mutagenesis studies in the future.
